How to Troubleshoot Crawled but Not Indexed

Google crawled but deemed not worth indexing, usually due to content quality, duplication, or thin content issues

What is "Crawled - currently not indexed"

"Crawled - currently not indexed" means Google has crawled the page content but decided not to add it to the index. This is more serious than "Discovered - not indexed" because Google actively chose not to index it after reviewing the content.

Common Causes

  • Insufficient content quality or thin content
  • Content duplicated or highly similar to other pages on the site
  • Page contains auto-generated low-value content
  • Lacking E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
  • Poor page user experience
  • Overall site quality score is not high

Troubleshooting Steps

  1. Confirm status and last crawl time in GSC URL Inspection
  2. Compare content differences between this page and indexed pages
  3. Check for duplicate content within the site
  4. Evaluate word count and information density (recommend over 800 words of substantial content)
  5. Check if the page has unique value
  6. Confirm the page has correct self-referencing canonical
  7. Check the actual rendered content after page load

Fix Methods

  • Significantly improve content quality: add original analysis, data, and case studies
  • Merge similar pages with 301 redirects to the best version
  • Add structured data to enhance page signals
  • Add author information and publish/update dates
  • Acquire external links to boost page authority

Verification

Request re-indexing after content optimization. If still not indexed after 2-4 weeks, consider whether the page should be merged into another page.
